# KPI Dashboard Specification v1.0

## Objective
The KPI Dashboard aims to provide a comprehensive overview of the performance metrics of the trading system, enabling stakeholders to assess operational efficiency, quality, reliability, and overall business performance.

## Consolidated KPIs
- **Operational KPIs**: Metrics related to trading volume, trade execution times, and system uptime.
- **Quality KPIs**: Measures of data integrity, transaction accuracy, and error rates.
- **Reliability KPIs**: Metrics on system availability, failover times, and recovery success rates.
- **Business KPIs**: Financial metrics such as profit margins, cost per trade, and customer acquisition costs.

## Dashboard Structure
1. **Executive Summary**: A high-level view of overall performance with key insights.
2. **Operations**: Detailed operational metrics concerning trading activities.
3. **Quality Gates**: Metrics governing the quality of data and processes.
4. **Incidents**: Summary of incidents affecting trading and system performance.

## Prometheus Metrics
- Define specific metrics to be collected from the trading system, such as latency, throughput, and error rates, to be visualized on the dashboard using Prometheus.

## Reporting Structure
- **Weekly Reports**: Overview of short-term operational performance.
- **Monthly Reports**: Detailed analysis of KPIs with trends and patterns.
- **Quarterly Reports**: Strategic review of long-term KPIs against business goals.

## Tech Lead Tasks
- Identify and implement metrics to be measured.
- Ensure proper setup of data collection and alerting systems.
- Oversee the development of dashboard visuals and reporting templates.

## Acceptance Criteria
- The dashboard meets user requirements for visibility and usability.
- Accurate and timely data representation according to the defined metrics.
